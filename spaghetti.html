Spaghetti

What is Spaghetti?spaghetti is a long, thin, cylindrical pasta of Italian origin. It is made from durum wheat semolina and water, and it is typically cooked by boiling. Spaghetti is one of the most popular types of pasta worldwide and is often served with various sauces, such as marinara, meat sauce, or pesto.

How to Cook Spaghetti

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions, usually about 8-12 minutes.
  3. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Once cooked, drain the spaghetti in a colander.
  5. Serve with your favorite sauce and toppings.
